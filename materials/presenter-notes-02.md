# Presenter notes - Session 2: Your AI chief of staff

Audience: senior management + C-level. The success metric is simple: every attendee leaves with a working morning briefing on their OWN calendar and inbox, plus a scheduled 8am run.

## Before the session (do not skip)
- CONNECTOR PREFLIGHT (the whole session hinges on this): confirm with the sys admin that Gmail/Calendar/Drive connectors are enabled for the exec team's Claude workspace, and whether the Claude app for Slack is installed. If connectors are NOT approved yet, get at least YOUR account enabled so every demo still runs live on the projector.
- Have your own Chief of Staff project already built and calibrated - your live briefing is the opening hook.
- Prepare the two-screen setup from the original playbook: Claude on one screen, Slack + Google Calendar on the other.
- Seed your inbox: send yourself one "URGENT: budget approval needed before board meeting" email an hour before, so triage has something real to catch.
- Print/queue the sys-admin request template - some execs will want to forward it during the session.

## Timing (45 min)
- 0-3 hook: run YOUR morning briefing live on the projector, cold. Let the room read a real one before any theory.
- 3-18 concepts, Live cards only:
  - Part 1 (6 min): architecture diagram, six-jobs table, security card (spend real time here - this room's first question is always security)
  - Part 2 (5 min): three-tier ladder, Tier 1 + Tier 2 cards (Tier 3 is one sentence: "your IT champion does this once, everyone benefits")
  - Part 3 (4 min): jobs 1-3 cards; the funnel diagram is the memorable visual
- 18-40 demos: Demo 1 (12 min, the main event) → Demo 2 (5 min) → Demo 3 (5 min)
- 40-45: Q&A + point at the follow-up path (ask Claude → #learn-claude → next session)

## Demo run-of-show
- Demo 1: narrate every click slowly - this audience won't say when they're lost. Pause at each step until most screens catch up. The VIP-list personalization moment is where engagement spikes: give 90 quiet seconds for people to type real names. If someone's connectors are blocked, pair them with a neighbor and mark it - that's their admin request.
- Demo 2: schedule the 8am task. Warn them: tomorrow's briefing will over-flag - that's normal, calibration is the homework.
- Demo 3: if Claude-in-Slack is NOT installed, do this as a presenter-only demo on your screen (or a 60-second video capture as backup) and close with "this is item 2 on the request you're sending IT today."
- Contingency: no wifi / connector outage → your pre-built project has yesterday's briefing in history; walk the thread instead of running live.

## The 15-minute exec one-on-one variant (from the original playbook)
For a single MD instead of a room: let THEM drive. 0-3 their briefing · 3-5 find a file · 5-8 book a meeting from Slack (the showstopper) · 8-11 room check · 11-13 show #cos-alerts · 13-15 the calibration pitch: "This is day 1. We calibrate the scoring together for two weeks - VIP list, thresholds, rooms. All on tools we already have."

## Q&A ammunition
- "Can it send emails for me?" - It drafts; you send. Deliberate Diligence boundary, and the room's compliance officer will thank you.
- "What does IT see?" - Admin console shows which connectors are enabled for whom; every action inherits YOUR permissions.
- "What if it misses a critical email?" - Week 1-2 calibration exists exactly for this; the rubric is yours to tune, and the on-demand check is always available as a second net.
- "Cost?" - Zero incremental for Tiers 1-2 (existing Teams + Workspace licenses). Tier 3 needs one API key: pennies per day at this volume.

## Cuts if running long
- Cut Demo 3 to presenter-only (60 seconds).
- Never cut the VIP personalization pause in Demo 1 - it's the difference between "nice demo" and "my chief of staff".
